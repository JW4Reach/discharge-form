STI Testing

  • To protect your health and prevent the potential spread of infection, it is strongly recommended that you remain abstinent from sexual activity until your test results are received and, if necessary, any treatment is completed.
  • Engaging in sexual activity during this time may compromise your health and will negate the results of this test, making them unreliable for diagnosis or treatment planning.

STD Results

  • Any further sexual activity could make these results invalid. Any further sexual activity with a non-exclusive partner with prior sexual exposure could make these results invalid. Exposure to an STI may occur, but depending upon the infection, may not test positive in the body for weeks or even years.
  • Condoms may help reduce the risk of an STI if used consistently and correctly each and every time, however they cannot provide 100% protection from an STI.
  • There are 20 million Americans that are infected with an STI each year. 50% of those are among the ages of 15–24 years old.

Avoiding Sexual Contact (oral, anal, vaginal) is the only 100% way to avoid an unintended pregnancy or a sexually transmitted infection (STI).
The choice is yours. Choose to know.

Negative Pregnancy Test

You received a negative pregnancy test result today. This may mean one of two things:

  • You are not pregnant.
  • You are pregnant, but it is too early to show on a urine pregnancy test or the test is not showing positive for some other reason

There are several possible reasons that you might miss a period, including but not limited to:

  • Sudden weight gain or loss (especially in extreme dieting, Anorexia and Bulimia)
  • Ovarian cysts Metabolic conditions (alterations in blood sugar and certain hormone levels)
  • Recent illness
  • Stress
  • Thyroid conditions
  • Certain birth control methods/medications
  • An early pregnancy
  • Breastfeeding

The Medical Staff and Director at Reach Reproductive Health recommends that you schedule an appointment with a physician to confirm that you are not pregnant and determine the reason for your missed period. We have provided a list of physician referrals for you. If you do not get your period within two weeks and have no symptoms, such as bleeding or pain, you can call us to set up an appointment for another pregnancy test if you wish.

  • Additionally, if you have not had a pap smear and/or STD testing in the last year, we strongly recommend that you schedule an appointment with a physician for these procedures.
  • The CDC recommends STD testing annually, in addition to every time you have a new sexual partner.

Chlamydia Treatment

MEDICATION: AZITHROMYCIN 1 gm

DIRECTIONS: You were given 4 tablets of AZITHROMYCIN 250 mg each (1 gm).

ABOUT YOUR MEDICINE:
AZITHROMYCIN (az-ith-roe-MYE-sin)
This medicine is a macrolide antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections.

PROPER USE OF THIS MEDICINE:
Follow the directions for using this medicine provided by your doctor. This medicine may be taken on an empty stomach or with food. DO NOT TAKE THIS MEDICINE within 1 hour before or 2 hours after aluminum- or magnesium-containing antacids. STORE THIS MEDICINE at room temperature, away from heat and light. To CLEAR UP YOUR INFECTION COMPLETELY, continue taking this medicine for the full course of treatment even if you feel better in a few days. Do not miss any doses. Taking this medicine at the same time each day will make it easier to remember. IF YOU MISS A DOSE OF THIS MEDICINE, take it as soon as possible. If it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and go back to your regular dosing schedule. If you miss a dose, do not take 2 doses at once.

PRECAUTIONS WHILE USING THIS MEDICINE:
If your symptoms do not improve within a few days or if they become worse, check with your doctor. IF MODERATE TO SEVERE DIARRHEA OCCURS during or after treatment with this medicine, check with your doctor or pharmacist. Do not treat it with non-prescription (over-the-counter) medicines. IF YOU EXPERIENCE difficulty breathing; tightness of your chest; swelling of eyelids, face, or lips; or if you develop a rash or hives, tell your doctor immediately or contact emergency care. Do not take any more doses of this medicine unless your doctor tells you to do so. BEFORE YOU BEGIN TAKING ANY NEW MEDICINE, either prescription or over-the-counter, check with your doctor or pharmacist. FOR WOMEN TAKING BIRTH CONTROL PILLS: this medicine may decrease the effectiveness of your birth control pill. To prevent pregnancy, you may want to use an additional form of birth control. IT IS UNKNOWN IF THIS DRUG IS EXCRETED IN BREAST MILK. IF YOU ARE OR WILL BE BREAST-FEEDING while you are using this medicine, check with your doctor or pharmacist to discuss the risks to your baby.

POSSIBLE SIDE EFFECTS OF THIS MEDICINE:
Side effects, that may go away during treatment, include mild diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. If they continue or are bothersome, check with your doctor. CHECK WITH YOUR DOCTOR AS SOON AS POSSIBLE if you experience hearing loss or ringing in the ears. If you notice other effects not listed above, contact your doctor, nurse, or pharmacist.

CONTACT YOUR PRIMARY CARE PHYSICIAN OR THE HOSPITAL EMERGENCY ROOM if you experience seizures or yellowing eyes and skin. If you notice other effects not listed above contact your doctor, nurse, or pharmacist.
